## Data Sources and Project Ideas

Below are five data sources I am considering for analysis, along with descriptions and ideas for how I could explore each dataset.

### 1. Professor Schueller’s Geolocation Data  
**Source:** (private dataset used in class)  
**Description:** GPS coordinates from 2020–2024 showing daily movement around Walla Walla and beyond.  
**Project Ideas:**  
- Analyze daily distance traveled  
- Compare weekday vs weekend movement  
- Determine most frequently visited locations  
- Seasonal trends and weather-related movement patterns  

---

### 2. NBA Shot and Game Data  
**Link:** https://www.nba.com/stats  
**Description:** Shot charts, play-by-play data, player statistics, and advanced metrics from NBA games.  
**Project Ideas:**  
- Shot distance vs accuracy models  
- Team-by-team shot efficiency heatmaps  
- Predicting win probability with logistic regression  
- Player clustering based on shooting behavior  

---

### 3. NOAA Weather & Climate Data  
**Link:** https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/  
**Description:** Historical records of temperature, rainfall, wind speed, and climate trends.  
**Project Ideas:**  
- Long-term trend analysis  
- Comparing regions or cities  
- Connecting weather patterns to movement (e.g., “Where’s Schueller?”)  
- Seasonal rainfall or temperature visualization  

---

### 4. World Bank Development Indicators  
**Link:** https://data.worldbank.org/  
**Description:** Worldwide economic, environmental, and social indicators across decades.  
**Project Ideas:**  
- Relationship between income and life expectancy  
- Interactive maps of global development  
- Clustering countries based on multiple indicators  
- Inequality comparisons across regions  

---

### 5. OpenStreetMap Points of Interest  
**Link:** https://www.openstreetmap.org/  
**Description:** User-generated geographic data including restaurants, parks, streets, and amenities.  
**Project Ideas:**  
- Mapping density of certain types of businesses  
- Comparing neighborhoods or regions  
- Access to grocery stores, parks, etc.  
- Overlaying POI data with geolocation data
